\section{Threats to Validity}\label{sec:threats}

\textbf{Single-run stochasticity.} Each task--agent--prompt cell was run once; aggregate patterns (Kubernetes 0/32, long-prompt SE-PASS lift, harness-tier ordering) are stable across all 62 tasks, but individual cell verdicts may shift on re-run. The deployment-relevant signal (SE-PASS rate of $\sim$1--2\%) sits well below typical re-run variance for these agents on bug-fix benchmarks.

\textbf{Product-level confound.} Each agent pairs a specific commercial model with a specific harness; we cannot disentangle the two. We therefore phrase observations such as ``harness leverage is substantial'' as observations on \emph{deployed products}, not as causal claims about harness-vs-model contribution. The 92.7\% SE-PASS-vs-non-PASS agreement between our judge panel and three blinded senior reviewers indicates that the verdicts themselves are not a panel artefact.

\textbf{Strict production-equivalence rubric.} Our rubric counts a run as SE-PASS only if it implements the reference behaviour and updates the same handwritten files (within the structural-equivalence rules in \S\ref{sec:study-design}). A valid alternative implementation that diverges from the upstream PR will be marked non-SE-PASS even if a human reviewer would accept it. The 1.0\% main-corpus headline therefore lower-bounds merge-readiness rather than measures it directly. A spot check of 30 PARTIAL cells (uniformly sampled) found roughly 4 cases where a senior reviewer would have accepted the agent's alternative; we therefore treat $\sim$3\% as a soft upper bound on agent-feasible merge rate under our task corpus.

\textbf{Calibration scope limitation.} By design, the 12-task calibration subset serves as the human-rater validation target; the 400 main-corpus runs are scored by the LLM panel alone. The 92.7\% SE-PASS-vs-non-SE-PASS agreement on the calibration subset transfers to the main corpus under the assumption that the panel's conservatism is task-family-independent. A blinded re-rate of a stratified sample of main-corpus runs is left as future work.

\textbf{T10 outlier sensitivity.} All four main-corpus SE-PASS outcomes concentrate on a single task (T10, a 1-file Presto benchmark addition). The 1.0\% headline becomes 0.0\% upon its exclusion. We retain T10 in the primary statistics because it was selected by the same curation funnel as all other CapBench tasks, but we report both inclusive and exclusive rates throughout.

\textbf{Single-run, single-prompt sampling.} Every cell of the 4 agents $\times$ 62 tasks $\times$ 2 prompts grid was run once. Stochastic decoding could move borderline runs across the SE-PASS threshold; the headline rates are therefore estimates, not tight bounds. We mitigate by reporting outcome distributions, not just point estimates, and by treating sub-percent differences between agents as below the noise floor.
