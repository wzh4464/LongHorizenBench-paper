\section{Related Work}\label{sec:related}

\paragraph{Coding-agent benchmarks.}
SWE-bench~\cite{jimenez2024swebench,chowdhury2024swebenchverified} established issue-resolution as the dominant paradigm. Extensions address data quality~\cite{aleithan2024swebenchplus,aleithan2025revisiting,badertdinov2025swerebench,prathifkumar2025memoryvability}, multilingual coverage~\cite{multiswebench,zan2024swebenchjava,joshi2025swebenchcl}, automated collection~\cite{guo2025swefactory,swebenchlive,xia2025livesweagent}, and broader surveys~\cite{li2026issuesurvey}. Feature-level and long-horizon benchmarks---SWE-bench Pro~\cite{swebenchpro2025}, SWE-EVO~\cite{sweevo}, FeatBench~\cite{featbench2025}, FeatureBench~\cite{zhou2026featurebench}, NL2Repo-Bench~\cite{ding2025nl2repo}, Web-Bench~\cite{xu2025webbench}---are closest to our setting; Production-derived benchmarks such as ProdCodeBench~\cite{prodcodebench2024} and SWT-Bench~\cite{mundler2024passerine} complement these with validation-focused and testing-focused workloads. CapBench differs in scale (median 57 files, 6{,}905~LOC) and criterion (production-equivalence SE-PASS rather than test-pass).

\paragraph{Coding-agent systems.}
Harness-engineering approaches---SWE-agent~\cite{harness2025}, Agentless~\cite{agentless}, AutoCodeRover~\cite{zhang2024autocoderover}, SWE-Fixer~\cite{xie2025swefixer}, Confucius~\cite{confucius}---and multi-agent designs---CodeR~\cite{multiagent2024}, SWE-Debate~\cite{li2025swedebate}, Self-Collaboration~\cite{dong2023selfcollab}, CoAct~\cite{song2025coact}, AgentSpawn~\cite{costa2026agentspawn}, daVinci-Agency~\cite{jiang2026davinci}---have driven rapid progress. Recent work explores experience-driven resolution~\cite{chen2025sweexp}, test-time scaling~\cite{gao2025trae}, and PR-based training~\cite{wang2025swedev,zhu2026prtraining}. We evaluate \emph{commercial products as deployed}, not research scaffolds.

\paragraph{Industrial reports and adoption.}
Production rollouts---Passerine~\cite{mathai2024passerine}, AutoBug~\cite{autobug}, LogSage~\cite{logsage}, Meta's test-gen pipeline~\cite{alshahwan2024testgen}, BitsAI-CR~\cite{codevisor}, and human-in-the-loop pipelines~\cite{takerngsaksiri2024hitl}---converge on a common finding: narrow tasks land, wide-scope work does not. GitHub adoption studies~\cite{watanabe2025agenticgithub,ehsani2026failedprs,robbes2026adoption,li2026aidev,pinna2026comparing,khan2026android,pham2026agenticprs} confirm lower acceptance rates and distinct change profiles for agentic PRs, with measurable security risks~\cite{zhao2025vibecodesafety}. Our work extends this to feature-PR-grade industrial work with a calibrated four-LLM panel and an anti-leakage protocol.

\paragraph{LLM-as-judge for code.}
Surveys~\cite{gu2024llmjudgesurvey,li2024llmsjudgessurvey,he2025llmjudgese,he2025courtroom} and SE-specific validations~\cite{crupi2025llmjudge,wang2025canllmsreplace,zhou2025llmjudgese,fandina2025automated,wu2024codeeval} show single judges are unreliable~\cite{thakur2024judgingjudges,jung2024trustescalate}; multi-judge panels improve stability~\cite{chen2025multiagentjudge,li2025metajudges,loi2025autobench} and domain calibration is essential~\cite{li2025webdevjudge,lu2024deepcrceval,weyssow2024codeultrafeedback,vo2025llmjudgebash,tseng2024codev}. Our four-LLM panel with conservative 3-of-4 tie-break, calibrated against blinded reviewers (92.7\% agreement), instantiates these findings as a production gate.
